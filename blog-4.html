HALTING THE $10B CAPITAL FLIGHT

Published on 17th February 2023 | Sharon


The $10B capital flight and data insecurity

Nigeria suffers from a lack of data security. Local service providers are unable to effectively store or safeguard data, because they possess outdated IT systems that do not support the latest data protection mechanisms. Companies are forced to store sensitive data on public clouds outside of Nigeria, with banks spending a conservative $10 billion yearly to store their sensitive data in public clouds outside Nigeria, through the services of Amazon Web Services (AWS), Azure, and Google Cloud Platform. In addition, offshore data storage causes Financial Service Institutions (FSIs) and other key organisations to invest millions of dollars annually in acquiring and managing IT infrastructure, instead of focusing on their core businesses.

However, a private-sector-led drive to end the massive capital battle and increase local content is on track. The game-changing partnership between Zadara, a United States (US)-based Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM), and Unitellas International Limited will halt the $10 billion capital expenditure (CapEx) by banks in Nigeria to store sensitive data in public clouds outside Nigeria.


How Unitellas solve the problems of data insecurity

This partnership will not only raise hopes of pushing back the huge capital flight, it will also help to boost the law on local content. Businesses and organisations storing their data on public clouds offshore, default on the guidelines on Local Content Law, which essentially requires organisations operating within Nigeria to have their data stored within the country.
